About Me

Originally from Dallas, TX, Jonathan is a Mexican-American oboist, international recitalist and recording artist. He has performed in recitals in Spain, Mexico City, and across the United States. He has taught at Northwestern University and the University of North Texas. His research is in Mexican music for the oboe, and he aims to perform works by underrepresented communities. He is the oboist for the exciting new music ensemble Fonema Consort, which focuses on presenting avant-garde works by Latin American composers for voice and ensemble.

He has also performed with various touring artists in Texas, including Josh Groban, Evanescence, and Il Divo.

Thompson has the following degrees:

  • Bachelor of Music from Northwestern University
  • Master of Music from Northwestern University
  • Doctor of Musical Arts from the University of North Texas

Tollan: Mexican Works for Oboe

Thompson was the recipient of the 2017–2018 Presser Graduate Award from UNT. With this grant, he recorded, produced, and released his premiere album "Tollan: Mexican Works for Oboe", featuring six previously unrecorded works by Mexican composers. The album was released June 2020 through Equilibrium Records.
